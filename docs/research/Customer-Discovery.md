# Customer Discovery

## Research Goal

Validate whether students and campus operators experience enough fragmentation in day-to-day university workflows to justify a unified campus operating layer with AI assistance.

This is portfolio-stage discovery, not formal commissioned research. The findings below should be read as lightweight validation and product synthesis used to shape the CampusOS case study.

## Research Methodology

| Source | Scope | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Informal student conversations | 10 students | Understand recurring campus workflow pain points |
| Informal faculty / advisor conversations | 3 faculty / advisors | Understand advising visibility and student-support gaps |
| Administrator-oriented discovery prompts | 3 target administrator profiles | Map likely operational concerns around queues, verification, and AI risk |
| Secondary research | University portals, student-service pages, campus app patterns, higher-education AI product research | Validate that fragmentation and student-service automation are active category problems |

### Methodology Notes

- This was not a statistically representative study.
- Frequency signals are directional and intended for product prioritization, not academic research claims.
- Administrator findings are framed as target-profile synthesis until deeper primary interviews are completed.
- The discovery goal was to identify MVP risks and product wedge candidates, not prove market demand conclusively.

## Target Participants

| Segment | Target sample | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Students | 10 | Primary users of campus workflows |
| Faculty / advisors | 5 | Need student context and academic visibility |
| Administrators | 3 | Own service queues, verification, and operations |

## Discovery Questions

### Students

- Which campus tasks are hardest to complete today?
- How many different portals or offices do you use in a normal week?
- What deadlines or service requests are easiest to miss?
- When would you trust an AI assistant, and when would you want a human?
- What would make a campus assistant feel reliable?

### Faculty / Advisors

- What student context do you wish you had before advising?
- Where do students repeatedly ask for help?
- What signals suggest a student may need intervention?
- What AI-generated recommendations would you trust or reject?

### Administrators

- Which workflows create the most repetitive manual requests?
- What data is missing when resolving student issues?
- How do you measure service quality today?
- What risks would block AI adoption?

## Early Pain Point Synthesis

| Problem | Frequency signal | Product implication |
|---|---:|---|
| Students cannot find the right campus resource | 8/10 students | AI copilot should start with service discovery and cited answers |
| Students use multiple disconnected portals | 7/10 students | Unified command center is a strong product wedge |
| Students miss deadlines or document tasks | 6/10 students | Priority tasks and proactive reminders are MVP candidates |
| Career and research resources feel fragmented | 9/10 students | Career hub belongs in roadmap, not first MVP |
| Faculty lack consolidated student context | 4/5 faculty | Advisor dashboard needs explainable academic signals |
| Admin teams repeat answers manually | 3/3 administrators | Ticket deflection is a core success metric |
| Staff worry about AI giving wrong policy answers | 3/3 administrators | Evaluation, citations, confidence thresholds, and handoff are required |

## Key Insights

1. The pain is not only information access. It is workflow completion across disconnected systems.
2. Students want speed, but administrators need trust, auditability, and escalation.
3. A campus AI assistant must be grounded in official policy and service data.
4. The strongest wedge is high-frequency service discovery plus closed-loop campus workflows.
5. Predictive advising and career matching are valuable, but should follow after trust is established.

## MVP Implications

| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Start with student command center | Students need one place for identity, tasks, services, and credentials |
| Add AI copilot with citations | AI trust depends on source-backed answers |
| Prioritize registrar, library, dining, and transport | These are frequent, visible workflows |
| Build admin intelligence dashboard | Operators need proof of reduced load and improved completion |
| Keep career hub in roadmap | Strong interest, but less urgent than service workflows |

## Validation Metrics

- Weekly active students completing workflows
- Ticket deflection with verified satisfaction
- Workflow completion rate
- Average time to complete document or service requests
- AI answer groundedness
- Escalation precision
- Admin dashboard weekly active users
