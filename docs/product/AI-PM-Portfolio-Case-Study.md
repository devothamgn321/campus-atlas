# AI Product Manager Portfolio Case Study

## Role

AI Product Manager for Campus Atlas, an AI-powered digital campus operating system for higher education.

## Category Context

Campus Atlas is a portfolio case study in an active market category. Higher education already has AI assistants, campus engagement platforms, chatbots, LMS integrations, and student-service automation products.

The project is not positioned as a first-of-its-kind invention. It is positioned as a demonstration of AI PM judgment: how to analyze an existing category, define a wedge, design differentiated workflows, evaluate AI quality, manage risks, and communicate a credible MVP path.

## Problem

Students interact with fragmented university systems: registrar portals, library tools, dining systems, transport desks, career portals, and manual help desks. This creates avoidable support load, slow verification, poor student experience, and weak operational visibility.

## Product Decision

The highest-leverage AI wedge is not a generic assistant. It is a campus-specific copilot connected to workflows, identity, verified data, and admin intelligence.

The differentiation is not "AI for campus" by itself. The differentiation is the combination of grounded answers, workflow completion, credential-aware identity, admin analytics, measurable AI quality, and responsible AI controls.
