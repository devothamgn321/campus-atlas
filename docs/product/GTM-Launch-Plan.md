# GTM And Launch Plan

## Target Segment

Start with universities that have:

- High manual student-service volume
- Fragmented portals across departments
- Pressure to improve student experience
- Existing digitization budget but limited AI product capability

## Beachhead Workflow

Registrar and student-service support are the best first wedge because they combine:

- High frequency
- Clear operational pain
- Measurable ticket reduction
- Strong expansion paths into identity, credentials, and admin analytics

## Launch Stages

| Stage | Timeline | Goal | Proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| Design Partner Pilot | 0-8 weeks | Validate registrar and student-service workflows with one university | 20% fewer manual tickets, 4.3/5 student satisfaction |
| Campus MVP | 2-4 months | Expand to identity, library, dining, and transport workflows | Six live workflows, credential verification MVP, 60% copilot resolution |
| Platform Expansion | 4-9 months | Add LMS, SSO, payments, career matching, and analytics exports | Multi-department adoption and paid expansion path |

## Buyer Narrative

Campus Atlas helps universities reduce student-service friction, modernize campus workflows, and introduce AI in a responsible, measurable way.

## Sales Motion

- Start with design partner pilots
- Sell to student affairs, registrar, and CIO stakeholders
- Prove value through manual ticket reduction and workflow completion
- Expand through identity, credentials, analytics, and integrations

## Demo Story

1. Student asks Copilot about a registrar workflow.
2. Copilot answers with cited policy and opens the correct action.
3. Student completes the workflow.
4. Admin dashboard shows reduced manual tickets and improved completion.
5. Credential verifier proves trust and record integrity.
