# Portfolio Story — Campus Atlas

## Origin

Campus Atlas began as a final-year engineering project (Student Link) that digitized student records, library services, canteen workflows, and basic campus administration.

Moving from engineering into product management, I revisited that project through a product strategy lens. The original system solved isolated tasks. The larger opportunity was a unified digital operating system for higher education — one that connects student identity, campus services, AI-assisted support, and administrative intelligence into a single product.

Campus Atlas is that reframed product: an AI-powered campus operating system, designed and documented the way I would approach it as an AI Product Manager.

## What Changed From the Original Project

- **Scope**: from a set of standalone campus tools to a connected platform with a defined wedge (AI copilot + admin intelligence) and an explicit MVP boundary.
- **Strategy**: every module is tied to a persona, a job to be done, and a success metric — not built because it was technically possible.
- **AI approach**: the copilot is treated as a product with its own evaluation plan, guardrails, and launch gates (groundedness, hallucination rate, escalation precision), not a feature bolted on at the end.
- **Positioning**: framed honestly as a case study in an existing market category, not a "first of its kind" claim — the goal is to demonstrate judgment, not novelty.

## What This Project Is Meant to Show

- I can take an ambiguous, broad problem space (campus operations) and narrow it to a defensible wedge.
- I can write the full PM artifact set — PRD, personas, journeys, metrics, experiments, GTM, roadmap — and keep them consistent with each other.
- I can apply AI PM rigor specifically: defining what "good" looks like for an AI feature, how to measure it, and when to escalate to a human instead of trusting the model.
- I can turn product decisions into a working prototype (React/Vite frontend, Express API) rather than stopping at slides.

## How I'd Talk About This in an Interview

Campus Atlas is a self-directed case study built to demonstrate how I'd operate as an AI Product Manager: take a real, recognizable problem (fragmented campus services), define a focused AI-driven wedge, back it with personas and evaluation criteria, and ship a working prototype that reflects those decisions. It's a portfolio piece, not a funded startup — and I'd talk about it as evidence of how I think and work, including the parts I'd still want to validate with real users before calling it MVP-ready.
