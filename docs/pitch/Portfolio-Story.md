# Portfolio Story — CampusOS

CampusOS began as my final-year engineering project, Student Link, which digitized student records, library services, canteen workflows, and campus administration modules.

After moving into Engineering Management and Product Management, I revisited the project from a product strategy lens. I realized the original idea had a larger product question: how should universities responsibly use AI to reduce fragmented student-service workflows while improving trust, measurement, and operational visibility?

CampusOS 2.0 is my upgraded AI PM case study.

It is not meant to claim that campus AI products do not exist. They do. The goal is to show how I would approach the category as an AI Product Manager: define the wedge, understand users, map workflows, design RAG use cases, set quality gates, plan experiments, manage responsible AI risks, and communicate a credible launch path.

It shows my ability to:
- Identify a real operational problem
- Reframe a technical project as a scalable product
- Define user personas and success metrics
- Think across engineering, operations, and product strategy
- Design an AI-era platform for higher education
- Define AI evaluation and responsible AI controls
- Translate product strategy into a working prototype
- Communicate roadmap and business value

This project represents my transition from software engineering execution to product leadership thinking.
